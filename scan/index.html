Executive summary

What the sweep found

  • ✦The six advertisers in the original teardown are not the big players. Nebula (asknebula.com) has run 37,000 ads since May 2024 — about ten times Lumius, the largest brand in that first pass. Astroline is at 17,800.
  • ✦The structural finding: burner-page fleets. Serious operators front ONE funnel domain with a fleet of disposable persona Pages. Astroline runs four ("Cosmic Compatibility", "Moonology Insight", "Ethan the Regressologist", "Zodiac Unveiled Daily"), each sending 30/30 of its ads to sub.astroline.today. Page-level stats therefore understate these businesses badly, and page-level longevity is meaningless — the pages are consumable, the domain is the asset.
  • ✦The category is enormous. For a single keyword in a single country the Ads Library reports ~13,000 active astrology ads in India, ~8,100 psychic ads in the US, ~7,600 soulmate ads in the US.
  • ✦Only two of six business models in this category are the one in the build blueprint. Most of the biggest spenders are pay-per-minute psychic-chat marketplaces staffed by human readers — a different business that needs a roster of live people.
  • ✦tryastro.org runs almost exactly the price ladder you picked — a $1 entry against a $49.99 recurring, on a soulmate-sketch quiz claiming "60M+ readings done", with an 8-minute countdown. It is the single closest live analogue to the funnel we would build.
  • ✦authorityastrology.com is already selling the AI-astrologer product — "Your Personal AI Astrologer", $19.97/mo (or $16.66/mo annual), "399,701+ users" — on a lifetime total of about 4 ads. It is an organic/SEO-led play, not a paid-media one. That is a real strategic alternative to buying traffic.
  • ✦hint.app runs the same palm-scan mechanic as Lumius, across two domains (hint.app + hintapp.com) and 7,400 lifetime ads, and loads OpenAI assets in the funnel.
  • ✦Longest continuously advertising: InstaAstro since Oct 2023, 7th Sense Psychics since Nov 2023, Spiritual Society since Jan 2024 — all still live today. Longevity in this category is normal; it is the fleet that makes it survivable.

§07

Reading the board

What the numbers mean for the build, and where this scan changes the plan.

The original six were a biased sample

The first teardown studied six Pages that happened to be visible from one starting point. This sweep searched 27 keywords across 28 countries and surfaced 1,097 distinct advertiser Pages, and it re-found four of the original six on its own — a good validation of the method, and a clear signal that the first pass caught the pattern but not the scale.

Ranked by lifetime ad volume, the original set lands mid-table. Nebula alone has run roughly ten times Lumius's output, and Astroline about five times, and both were entirely absent from the first pass.

Why page-level numbers lie

The single most useful thing this scan found is not a brand, it is a structure. Astroline's four Pages look like four small unrelated advertisers — a compatibility brand, a moon brand, a past-life-regression persona, a daily-zodiac brand. All four point every ad at sub.astroline.today. Psychic Samira runs two Pages plus per-geo subdomains (es., it.). Hint runs two Pages across two domains. Spiritual Society runs .co and .com side by side.

This is the ad-account-ban risk from the build blueprint, solved in production. The operators assume Pages will die and build so that losing one costs them nothing. It directly validates the vantage + Gonzo isolation plan — but it also says the isolation has to extend to Pages and personas, not just ad accounts and browser fingerprints.

The models are not interchangeable

From the creative alone the category looks like one business. It is six. The biggest spender in the whole scan, Nebula, is a human psychic-chat marketplace — "your first 3 minutes are free", then per-minute credits. Its footer names Yolo Brothers Inc., 500 North Rainbow Blvd, Las Vegas while its own telemetry goes to sentry.obrio.net and analytics-proxy.obrio.net, so the operator is the OBRIO app group behind the front company. That business needs staffed readers; its ad volume is not evidence that our model works.

The brands that do run our model — Astroline, Hint, tryastro.org, plus the already-traced Lumius / SkyWhisper / Aspectica — are all bespoke Next.js funnels on web Stripe, several on Supabase, exactly the architecture already specified in the blueprint. tryastro.org even shares Lumius's Supabase-backed stack.

The counter-example worth taking seriously

Authority Astrology sells the product we intend to build — a personal AI astrologer, monthly forecasts, chart readings, $19.97/mo with a free trial — and has bought almost no ads at all in two years. Its stack is PostHog, Stape server-side tagging and Crazy Egg, which is an SEO/CRO posture, not a media-buying one. Whether that is a better business than a paid quiz funnel is unknown from the outside, but it is the one live example of the same product with the opposite go-to-market, and it is cheap to study.

§08

Recommended next moves

In order, with the reason each one earns its place.

  1. Trace Astroline end-to-end (sub.astroline.today/quiz-pp). Biggest true analogue to what we would build, 4-Page fleet, 28 geos, 12.9 months live. Walk it to the paywall in two geos to get the ladder and the A/B variants, same method as the Lumius traces.
  2. Trace tryastro.org (/flows/g). It runs the $1 → $49.99 ladder you chose, so its paywall is effectively a reference implementation of our own pricing decision.
  3. Trace Hint (hint.app/palmistry). Confirms whether the palm-scan step lifts conversion enough to justify the friction — the one open question from the original blueprint that a trace can actually answer.
  4. Read Authority Astrology's funnel and pricing page properly. Same product, opposite acquisition model, on ~4 lifetime ads. Cheapest possible lesson available.
  5. Decide the Page-fleet policy before spending a cent. Every operator at scale here runs disposable persona Pages against one durable funnel domain. If we launch with a single Page we are one ban from zero.
  6. Do not copy Nebula, 7th Sense, Esmeralda, Kang or Chave Mística. They are per-minute human-reader marketplaces. Their ad volume is impressive and irrelevant to us.
§09

What these numbers are — and are not

Read these before quoting anything above.

  1. Lifetime ad counts are the Ads Library's own reported totals for each advertiser Page (country=ALL, active_status=all), read back out of the saved capture and re-checked against the raw HTML. They count ad creatives, not spend, not revenue. A brand running many cheap creative variants will out-score a brand running few expensive ones.
  2. Brand totals only sum the fronting Pages this sweep actually found. If a brand runs Pages that none of the 238 searches surfaced, its true total is higher. Every figure here is a floor, never a ceiling. SkyWhisper's low total is the clearest example — it almost certainly advertises from Pages this sweep did not see.
  3. Geo counts mean targeting breadth, not ad volume. One ad targeting 22 countries appears in 22 country searches. Authority Astrology shows 22 geos on ~4 lifetime ads for exactly this reason. Read the geo column as reach, not spend.
  4. is_active in the raw data is not reliable on its own — some records carry is_active: true with an end date a year in the past. Everything on this page uses "end date is today" instead, which is why a few brands that look active in a naive read do not appear here.
  5. The sweep sees only the first results batch per search. Facebook rate-limits GraphQL pagination from a datacenter IP, so each of the 238 searches contributed roughly its top 30 ads. Discovery breadth is good; per-search depth is deliberately shallow.
  6. 2 of 238 searches returned nothing — IT "psychic" and GR "wroskopio" (a bad Greek transliteration; the correct term is ωροσκόπιο). Both were retried on a residential exit and still returned zero. Greek and Italian coverage is therefore slightly thinner than the other geos.
  7. Destination checks were read-only. Each brand's advertised URL was fetched once and its landing state recorded. No quiz was completed, no form submitted, no checkout reached, no card ever entered — so prices shown are only those visible on the landing page, not full paywall ladders. Getting those requires the full walk-to-paywall trace used in the original teardown.
  8. Prices are landing-page text, not verified checkout terms. They are indicative. Treat any figure here as a lead to confirm by tracing, not as a quotable price.
